Research

I am passionate about AI, Machine Learning, and Computer Vision, and I aim to develop data-driven solutions that support sustainable development. My focus is on making machine learning models more transparent, reliable, and trustworthy for real-world applications.

One of my key contributions is a machine learning-based method for detecting adulterated oil, helping to improve transparency and safety in the energy sector. I have also worked on several practical projects. At the Federal University Lokoja, I developed a GPA prediction system that not only forecasts student performance but also explains the factors influencing it. Additionally, I built a deep learning-based road anomaly detection system that uses Computer Vision to identify infrastructure issues, making road maintenance more efficient and improving safety.

Publications

  • A computer-vision based system for detecting road anomalies in Nigeria. : Ahmad Khalid*, Temitope Oluwafemi. (Manuscript under preparation.)

Completed Projects

  • Road Anomaly Detection System: Developed a machine learning model to detect road surface anomalies (e.g., potholes, cracks) using image data with CNNs, deployed with Flask.
  • GPA Prediction System: Built a machine learning model to predict student GPAs based on academic data, using Linear Regression, Decision Trees, and Random Forest models.

Ongoing Project

  • Intelligent System for the Classification of West African Currency: Developing an AI-based system using computer vision and machine learning to accurately identify and classify the country of origin based on West African currencies, with the goal of facilitating trade, tourism, and economic activities between countries in the region, while reducing the risk of errors in currency exchange..
  • Exploring the Impact of Masked Autoencoders in Imaging: Investigating how masked autoencoders can improve feature learning, image reconstruction, and anomaly detection in computer vision tasks.
